9. what is the meaning of LEAVE NO TRACE POLICY in conducting outdoor activities?


Answer:

LEAVE NO TRACE POLICY

This policy applies to campers and hikers that visit sites for camping and trail. This policy wants to preserve and protect the natural sites without preventing people to enter. This means that it can still accept visitors but the natural habitats of animals could be the same as it is.

The following are the seven principles of the LEAVE NO TRACE POLICY.

Plan before the trip. Knowing the regulations of the site beforehand lessen the chances of complicated situations you are going to encounter. Less complications also helps in leaving no traces in the siteBe mindful of the chosen campsites. Choosing an excellent site where the animals and plants will not be harmed or disturbed is very important. It also adds to the safety of the campers. Waste management. Traces could be in forms of wastes. Since we should exhibit no traces, then we should dispose our garbages properly. Leave what you find. Traces could also be from getting anything from the site. It means that its not what it is anymore. Even it is just a small rock, you can take pictures but never take it. Be careful with fire. Camping is fun with setting up campfires and chat with everyone. But be mindful of controlling fire, make sure it is fully distinguished before leaving. And make the fire impact minimal for the living organisms in the surrounding.Respect creatures of the wild. Natural sites would have these creatures and as long as you do not disturb or make fun of them (or even taking them home) then you still leave no traces. Respect other campers/visitors. It is a recreational activity most of the time. Respecting the experience of other people is also one of the policies.
