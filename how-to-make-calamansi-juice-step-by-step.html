2. write the steps on how to make calamansi juice using signal words​


Answer:

To make calamansi juice, you will need the following ingredients and equipment:

Ingredients:

Fresh calamansi fruitsSugar or honey (optional)Water or ice

Equipment:

Juicer or citrus squeezerPitcher or glass

Step-by-Step Guide:

First, wash the calamansi fruits thoroughly with water to remove any dirt or debris.

Cut the calamansi fruits in half with a sharp knife, being careful not to cut yourself.

Squeeze the juice from the calamansi halves using a juicer or citrus squeezer. If you don't have a juicer or squeezer, you can also extract the juice by hand using a fork or spoon.

Collect the extracted juice in a pitcher or glass.

If you prefer a sweeter juice, you can add sugar or honey to taste. Start with a small amount and adjust to your preference.

Dilute the calamansi juice with water or ice, depending on your preference. You can add more or less water depending on how strong you want the juice to be.

Stir the juice well until the sugar or honey is fully dissolved.

Serve the calamansi juice chilled or over ice. Enjoy your refreshing and healthy drink!

Explanation:

Signal words used in this step-by-step guide include "first," "next," "cut," "squeeze," "collect," "add," "dilute," "stir," and "serve." These signal words help to organize the steps in a logical sequence, making it easy to follow and understand the process of making calamansi juice.

19. Write the steps or procedure in preparing calamansi juice.​


Answer:

1.Wash the calamansi and drain.

2.Cut the upper portion taking care not to cut the seeds. Cut seeds contribute to an astringent taste.

3.Manually squeeze or use a fruit extractor.

4.Add one and 3/4 part sugar for every part water. Or adjust to 60 degree brix if you own a refractometer.

5.If a clear juice is desired, the so-called calamansi nip. Allow it to stand for three days (inside the refrigerator) until the fruit pulp has floated.

28. How to make 30% solution of calamansi juice? ​


Answer:

I will show my working in case someone reviews the answer. Typical lemon juice has a pH of 2.

So I'll work out how much citric acid I need in a litre of water to give me that pH. The pKa1 value is a measure of acidic strength and for citric acid this is 3.14 (I ignore pKa2 and pKa3 as they are negligible).

pH = 1/2(pKa - LogA) where A = concentration of citric acid.

2 = 1/2(3.14 - logA)

2 = 1.57 - 1/2logA

logA = -0.86

A = 0.14 mol/litre

Mr = 210.14

So concentration of citric acid = 0.14 x 210.4 = 29.4 g/litre

So ~ 30g in 1litre of distilled water should do it.
