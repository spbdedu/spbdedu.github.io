CONTEXT CLUES

The meanings of new or unfamiliar words can be deduced from context clues that can be discovered inside a sentence, paragraph, or passage.

His behavior was so ABOHERRENT that she felt she had no choice but to seek RECOMPENSATE from the company that allowed the behavior to continue.

Here is the supporting details:

An adjective that describes someone's nasty or hateful disposition is abhorrent. The assumption made in the question is that the male staff members have bad personalities, which causes the woman to feel unsettled or uneasy.

Therefore, the lady requests payment or accountability from the business that employs male employees. Recompense is the name for this woman's actions.

The first blank in the text can therefore be filled with repulsive, and the second blank with compensation.

Additional information:

Synonyms, antonyms, word structure hints, parallels (such metaphors and similes), and contrasts are further examples of context cues. For instance, context hints for synonyms present words nearby that have the same meaning.

The three ways that others explain context cues are:

For instance, the reader will anticipate that words connected with cats will be present in a novel about cats. These are semantic or meaning hints.

Word order inside a phrase can reveal the part of speech that a missing word must belong to, according to syntactic indicators (for example, a verb).

Picture hints: Beginning readers are taught to examine illustrations to aid in the recognition of a word from an early age.
