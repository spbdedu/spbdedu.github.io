22. Test #2: 20 pts Solve the following problem involving simple interest. Show your solution. Each problem is 10 points. Solution: I = PxRxT 1. Franz Norman borrowed Php 8,000 to his Brother Bj to buy a new phone. His brother gave him an interest of 2% to be paid in 10 months. How much money should Bj be paid back?


Answer:

To find out how much money Franz should pay back to Bj, you can use the simple interest formula: I = P x R x T

Where:

I = Interest

P = Principal (the original amount borrowed)

R = Interest rate (expressed as a decimal)

T = Time (in years)

In this case:

P = Php 8,000

R = 2% = 0.02 (converted to a decimal)

T = 10 months = 10/12 years (converted to years)

Plugging in the values:

I = Php 8,000 x 0.02 x (10/12)

I = Php 1,333.33

So, Franz should pay back a total of Php 8,000 + Php 1,333.33 = Php 9,333.33
