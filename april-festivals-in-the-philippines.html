15. G.R. No. L-14752 April 30, 1963 CARINO VS. PEOPLE OF THE PHILIPPINES


Answer:

G.R. No. 114270 January 20, 1995 ALCANTARA VS. COURT OF APPEALS

Both cases are decided by the Supreme Court of the Philippines and involve criminal matters. However, the Carino case was decided in 1963, while the Alcantara case was decided in 1995, showing a significant gap in time between the two cases.

In terms of the parties involved, Carino involves the defendant Carino and the People of the Philippines, while Alcantara involves the petitioner Alcantara and the Court of Appeals.

Carino is a case about illegal possession of firearms, where Carino's defense was that he was authorized to carry a gun as a forest ranger. The court ruled that possession of firearms without the appropriate license is illegal, even if the defendant had other justifications for carrying a gun.

In contrast, Alcantara involves a charged of frustrated homicide against the petitioner, who argued that he was acting in self-defense. The court ruled that while self-defense may be a valid defense, it is the petitioner's burden to prove that he was actually in danger and that his actions were only meant to defend himself.

Overall, while both cases involve criminal issues and the decision of the Supreme Court of the Philippines, they differ in terms of the specific crime charged and the issues raised by the defendants.

17. the event happen on april 1, 1521 from the time of magellan here in the philippines? ​


Answer:

On April 27, 1521, Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan was killed by a poison arrow during a skirmish on the island of Mactan in what is now the Philippines. Magellan and his crew were assisting a local king they had allied with when they landed on the island of Cebu weeks earlier.

Magellan and his crew endured a long voyage to the Philippines. First, they sailed from Spain to the Canary Islands off the coast of West Africa. From there, they crossed the Atlantic Ocean to what is now Brazil and Argentina. The harrowing trip around the tip of South America—what is now called the Strait of Magellan—took 38 days. Then the crew set sail across the Pacific. By the time Magellan and his crew reached the Philippines, they had been at sea for more than a year.

Magellan was on a route to Portuguese territories in the Spice Islands (now Indonesia). Although Magellan himself was Portuguese, he was working for Spain, and hoped to claim the lucrative islands for the Spanish Empire.

19. What happened on the day of April 27 1521 in the Philippines


Answer:

Battle of Mactan

Explanation:

The Battle of Mactan (Cebuano: Gubat sa Mactan; Filipino: Labanan sa Mactan) was a fierce clash fought in the Philippines on 27 April 1521. The warriors of Lapulapu, one of the Datus of Mactan, overpowered and defeated a Spanish force fighting for Rajah Humabon of Cebu under the command of Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan, who was killed in the battle. The outcome of the battle resulted in the temporary departure of the Spanish crew from the archipelago.

The Philippines has five types of climates: tropical rainforest, tropical monsoon, tropical savanna, humid subtropical and oceanic (both are in higher-altitude areas) characterized by relatively high temperature, oppressive humidity and plenty of rainfall.
