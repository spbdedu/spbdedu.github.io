5. It is an elaborate curvilinear carved motif made by the mars nao and tausug tribes in mindanao. a. WeavingB. Okir-a-datuC. CrocodileD. InabelE. LightningF. BulolG. BinetwaganH. Pina and abaca fiberI. SarimanokJ. BinakulK. Diamond ​


Answer:

B. Okir-a-datu

Explanation:

Okir-a-datuare elaborate curvilinear carved motifs made by the Maranao and Tausug tribes of Mindanao. The main okir motifs are the sarimanok (mythical bird), the naga (mythical serpent) and the pako rabong (fern). Such motifs are used to decorate the houses of Sultans.

8. what are the facts and characteristics of bulol​


Answer:

The shoulders are broad while the torso appears as a tapering triangular form. The erect phallus is carved in relief on the lower abdomen. The legs are muscular with the knees slightly bent and the feet are rendered as thin, plank-like forms. The toes are delineated with incised notches and the toenails are indicated.

20. what is message of bulol​


Answer:

Bulol are the most numerous and best known of Ifugao figurative sculptures and usually take the form of either a standing or seated figure. They are carved from a single piece of wood and generally exhibit a stylized and geometric rendering of the human body.

25. What is kahit bulol magsalita ako??​


Answer:

"Kahit bulol magsalita ako" is a Filipino phrase that means "Even though I speak stutteringly" or "Even if I speak with a stutter". It's often used as an expression of determination to communicate despite having difficulty speaking clearly.

27. characteristics of bulol Philippine art form a​


Answer:

A Bul ul has a simplified shape of a human being, whether male or female.

Explanation:

It consists of a simplified head, a torso, and a pair of hands and legs mounted on a platform for stability. It is carved out of strong narra or ipil wood and sometimes stone. Sizes also vary, depending on its use.
