[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

In a bag of red and white onions, the ratio of red onions is to white onions is 3:4. If the bag contains 120 white onions, how many red onions are there?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Write the proportion and let x be the number of red onions compared to 120 white onions equal to 3:4.

[tex] x \::\: 120 = 3 \::\: 4 [/tex]

» Multiply the means and the extremes.

[tex] 4x = 120(3) [/tex]

[tex] 4x = 360 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{\,4x\,}{4} = \frac{\,360\,}{4} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = 90 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 90 red onions in the bag.

15. demonstrate and describe how to regenerate green onions by following the procedure​


Answer:

Stand the bulbs root-end down in a small jar. (I stood them up in an egg cup.) ...

Next slice off the ends of the bulbs, leaving roots attached.

Next Stand the bulbs root-end down in a small jar. (I stood them up in an egg cup.) Add enough water to cover the roots, but leave the top edges above water.

Then, Set on a windowsill and keep the roots moist. After a few days, green shoots will emerge from the tops of the bulbs. After that, they'll grow very quickly.

Then, Keep the roots submerged and change water at least once a week.

Green Onions Rooted in Water Photo by Vanessa Greaves.

Next, When the shoots are or four or five inches long, you can plant them in the ground or a pot filled with good quality potting soil. Repotting is important because if you just keep the root ends in the jar, they will produce green shoots for a while but the plant will weaken eventually and stop producing.

Then, Snip off what you need, cutting the leaves all the way to the ground; the onions will continue to grow again from the cut end. If you don't cut the greens down to the ground, the plant could get to be much larger than the green onions you find in your grocery store. If they flower, you can use the flavorful blossoms in salads

Finally, Depending on where you live (I'm in the Philippines) your green onions in the ground can survive winter, but since Pilippines is a tropical country much like some of your hardier kales and other wintertime greens. They'll start perking up around springtime, which is when I start putting new starts into the ground to replace anything that looks a little weary.

Now you'll always have fresh green onions on hand for recipes and garnishes any time you want them.

24. What is your conclusion about Regrowing the Green onion?​


Answer:

After 10 days of testing the experimental groups we were able to prove the hypothesis correct. The hypothesis originally stated that the onions in soil would have a better and healthier growth than the onions in the other experimental groups (water, vinegar and fertilizer). As the experiment developed, we observed greens spuds germinating from the tip of the onions in soil. We also recorded the changes and results of the other experimental groups. With these two pieces of information we were able to create a comparison that proved the hypothesis correct. 

After analyzing the results of the experiment we noticed two experimental errors:

First, time was not properly used. Seven day was not an adequate duration for this experiment. A variation for a second trial would be to increase the amount of time the tests groups are exposed to change. 

Second experimental error was not taking into consideration the amount of liquid in the experimental group and the weight of the onions. Without this data we were not able to record specific information about the quantity of water absorbed by the onions. In a second trial of this experiment we will make sure to initially record this measurements so that we can get accurate results about the absorption of liquids by the green onions.
