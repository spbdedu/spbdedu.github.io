A vasectomy is a procedure that makes a man permanently unable to get a woman pregnant. It involves cutting or blocking two tubes, called the vas deferens inside the scrotum. This prevents sperm from getting into the semen.

6. Vasectomy is a process wherein the vas deferens is cut or tired. How can this procedure prevent pregnancy?


A vasectomy blocks or cuts each vas deferens tube, keeping sperm out of your semen. Sperm cells stay in your testicles and are absorbed by your body. Starting 3 months after a vasectomy, you semen won't contain any sperm, so it can't cause pregnancy.
