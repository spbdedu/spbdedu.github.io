10. what is the meter of the song "leron leron sinta"?


The Time Signature for Leron leron Sinta is 2/2.

Explanation:

Time Signature is the term that is use to determine how a certain song is to be counted.

It is often found in Music notations right after the cleff and key signature and is written similarly like a fraction.

Just like the following :

2/2

2/4

4/4

3/2

The upper part of the time signature determines the number of beats in one Measure,

The lower part of the time signature determines what kind of notes to count in one measure.

If you will sing Leron-leron Sinta you will notice that you can count 1 - 2 in every measure or bar of the song. That is because of its 2/2 time signature .

Answer:

Pamulinawen and Leron-Leron Sinta are two traditional Filipino folk songs.

"Pamulinawen" is a love song from the Ilocano region of the Philippines and is sung in the Ilocano language. It is a slow and romantic melody that tells of a man's devotion and admiration for a woman named Pamulinawen.

"Leron-Leron Sinta" is a traditional Tagalog folk song, originating from the central part of Luzon island in the Philippines. It is a playful and upbeat melody that is typically sung during community gatherings and celebrations. The song's lyrics revolve around a young woman named Leron and the suitors who compete for her love.

In summary, "Pamulinawen" is a slower, romantic love song while "Leron-Leron Sinta" is a lively, upbeat folk song.
