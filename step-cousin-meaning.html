5. Your cousin visited you in your house, Your cousin enters your bedroom and borrowed earphone without any permission. Do you think, your cousins actions violates the original meaning of familism or violates the principle of "delicadeza"?​.


Answer:

It seems that your cousin's actions may have violated the principle of "delicadeza," which is a Spanish term that refers to the quality of being polite, considerate, and respectful towards others. Taking someone's personal belongings without their permission is generally seen as rude and disrespectful, and would not be considered an act of "familism," which is the value placed on loyalty and commitment to one's family. Familism generally involves showing care and support towards one's family members, not taking actions that might be perceived as disrespectful or inconsiderate.

7. Some sixty years later, an elderly Frances Griffiths publicly admitted that her and her cousin had staged the photographs as a practical joke. A.NO CHANGE B.her cousin and herself C.she and her cousin C.her cousin and her


Answer:

C.

Explanation:

One trick to use, when you’re looking a compound noun (two nouns or pronouns connected with the word "and"), is to take the noun away, leave the pronoun, and see whether the sentence still works.   Here, we would get: "Frances Griffiths admitted that her had staged the photographs,” which is clearly wrong. This means answers A and D are out – both use the “her” form of the pronoun. Using the trick with answer B, we would get: “Frances Griffiths admitted that herself had staged the photographs,” which also doesn’t work. The only answer that works is C, which uses the subject form of the pronoun: “she.”C-she and her cousin
