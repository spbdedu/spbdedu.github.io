17. What I Can Do Answer the questions below: A. What are the issues being implied in these lines of the poem- 1. Death, rather than servitude, 2. a revolt has broken out 3. I wish I were free and could take part in enemy's rout. B. Are these issues present in our country? Why do you say so?​


Answer:

These lines of the poem imply several issues related to freedom, rebellion, and death.

"Death, rather than servitude" suggests that the speaker values their freedom above all else and would prefer to die rather than live in servitude or slavery. This line highlights the importance of freedom and the sacrifices people are willing to make to preserve it.

"A revolt has broken out" indicates that there is unrest and rebellion among the people. This line suggests that the speaker is aware of the political situation around them and that they are possibly in the midst of a rebellion or revolution. It also suggests that the speaker might be considering joining the revolt to fight for their freedom.

"I wish I were free and could take part in the enemy's rout" implies that the speaker desires to be free and take part in the enemy's defeat. This line suggests that the speaker may not have the opportunity to join the fight due to their current situation.

These issues are present in many countries around the world, including in some parts of India. The desire for freedom, the presence of rebellion or revolution, and the willingness to sacrifice one's life for freedom are universal themes that have been present throughout history. In India, there have been various struggles for freedom and independence, including the Indian Independence Movement, which fought against British colonialism. These struggles involved sacrifice and revolt, and the desire for freedom and the right to self-determination is still relevant today in many parts of the country.
