6. Have you ever gotten (or give) a ''Compliment'' that really wasn't a compliment? How did you feel afterwards?


I've received compliments that didn't really compliment at all. And my feeling afterward was offended because it was bad behavior.

Backhanded Compliment in the Merriam–Webster dictionary is defined as a compliment which implies that the complement is not really a compliment at all.

Without realizing it, this term gives the meaning of satire that is inserted in a compliment. Someone who throws such a false compliment clearly does not mean to praise but insinuates subtly, then an ambiguous connotation arises for the person who receives it.

Someone who likes to give these fake compliments usually feels threatened by your good fortune isn't completely happy with your success or accomplishments, or even they don't really like you. Therefore, they give unreasonable compliments and are always looking for blame to subtly bring you down.

Here are 5 examples of backhanded compliment sentences:

“I thought one of you could pass, Congrats!!"

It's a compliment, but by underestimating someone's ability. They think that you are not really smart, when in fact you are capable of achieving that feat. This should actually be a good inspiration and motivation for them.

“Originally, you are really cool and confident! If I might be stressed I have a spotty face like that!”

You must have heard this sentence very often, both on social media and in the environment around us. It would be better if we appreciate someone who has a high level of self-confidence and self-love, without having to emphasize their shortcomings.

“Wow, are you serious about being accepted into EY? You know a lot of people inside, don't you!"

Accusations wrapped in praise, right? who always says something like this? Believe me, maybe for some people this is a normal thing. However, praise to hurt other people's feelings is not a good thing you know! We never know the effort and hard work that this person put in to be able to work and be accepted in EY.

"When will I have a girl who doesn't look physically like your girl!"

Consciously or not, this compliment leads to body shaming, you know! Frontally, the person said that his friend had a bad physique. A person's self-confidence cannot be measured by physical form, you guys, of course, there are many other choices of words to mourn your single fate without hurting anyone's feelings.

“I swear your photo is so beautiful! What filter is it using?”

At first glance, this sentence expresses a compliment on a girl's appearance that is very beautiful, but it is followed by the words that she looks beautiful because she uses a filter.
