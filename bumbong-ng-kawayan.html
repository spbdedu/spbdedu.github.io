28. how will you help to preserve the traditional musical instruments such as Bamboo Organ, Musikong Bumbong, Angklung Ensemble and Pangkat Kawayan? ​


Answer:

The Las Piñas Bamboo Organ in St. Joseph Parish Church in Las Piñas, Philippines, is a 19th-century church organ. It is known for its unique organ pipes; of its 1031 pipes, 902 are made of bamboo. It was completed after 6 years of work in 1824 by Father Diego Cera, the builder of the town's stone church and its first resident Catholic parish priest.


29. How are the distinct musical of Rondalla, Musikong Bumbong, Himig Pangkat Kawayan, Angklung Ensemble and Bamboo Organ perform


Answer:

The rondalla is an ensemble of stringed instruments played with the plectrum or pick and generally known asplectrum instruments. It originated in Medieval Spain, especially in Catalonia,[1] Aragon, Murcia, and Valencia. The tradition was later taken to Spanish America and elsewhere. The word rondalla is from the Spanish ronda, meaning "serenade."

A brass band is a musical ensemble generally consisting entirely of brass instruments, most often with a percussion section. Ensembles that include brass and woodwind instruments can in certain traditions also be termed brass bands (particularly in the context of New Orleans–style brass bands), but may more correctly termed military bands, concert bands, or "brass and reed" bands.

The bands, whose name means "bamboo music", give marching tunes a distinctly Asian twist, using the grass (bamboo) which is indigenous in South

The Las Piñas Bamboo Organ in St. Joseph Parish Church in Las Piñas City, Philippines, is a 19thcentury church organ with unique organ pipes; they are made almost entirely of bamboo. It was completed in 1824 by Father Diego Cera, the builder of the town's stone church and its first resident Catholic parish priest

The angklung is a musical instrument made from bamboo and tuned to the different notes of the scale. A musical note is sounded upon shaking the instrument and music is made when several of the instruments are shaken together.

Pangkat Kawayan, popularly known as the “Singing Bamboos of the Philippines,” is an orchestra that plays music using nontraditional bamboo instruments. Founded in 1966, it is always composed of young students aged eight to 19 selected from the schools of Quezon City and Manila.
