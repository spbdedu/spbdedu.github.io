Questions: a. Identify and narrate at least one instance in the past when women were relegated in Philippine political life. b. The author said, "Women and men do not enjoy the same access to resources that yields power." While this is true today, identify and narrate at least one instance in the past when women yielded power in Philippine society.


Answer:

a. In the past, women in the Philippines were relegated in Philippine political life due to the fact that they were often excluded from decision-making roles, or those which could bring them political power. This was a result of oppressive gender roles that governed the patriarchal society, where men wielded the majority of the power. For example, women were not allowed to vote until after the Second World War and until the Philippine Constitution of 1987.

b. One instance in the past when women yielded power in Philippine society was during the reign of Joséphine de Beauharnais, who was the first female Governor-General of the Philippines. She was an intelligent and influential leader who made significant strides for women's rights during her political period in the 1800s. She championed women's educational efforts, repeal of oppressive policies that suppresses women's economic opportunities, and provided greater access to political rights for women. She made a bold statement for the era by being the first female leader in the region.
