10. What is the meaning of liberating education through innovation


The meaning of “liberating education through innovation” is “freeing education through transformation” in simpler terms.  

Education has long been part of this world. Currently, our world is already experiencing continuous transformations through technology and globalization. Embracing innovation in education system will enable education to reach greater heights that will enable students and teachers to improve for the better. It is only through openness to good changes will education be able to cope up with the swift improvements of our world.  

Current examples of innovations in education is the setting up of online classes in the internet. Before, attending school is only confined in the four walls of the classroom. Now, anyone can already learn online through online classes, without the need to go to school physically. This shows that education was able to cope up and maximize the wide usage of the internet anywhere in the world. Moreover, this milestone is a true manifestation that education is truly for anyone, regardless where they are or how they learn.  

Another example of the role of innovation in education is the usage of Youtube materials during class discussions. Before, teachers only use the blackboard, Manila paper or overhead projector to discuss a topic more clearly. Now, teachers and professors already use education Youtube videos to catch the attention of their students, and let the students understand the topics more.  

I’m sure you will be able to cite more examples of innovation in education that exist in your school or classroom.

17. Liberation and Human Promotion meaning


Answer:

Liberation theology encouraged a break from an elitist notion of the Church and the return of control to the people.  By involving the poor in their own liberation and offering Christianity as a tool towards a more perfect society, liberation theologians dramatically changed the relationship between not only the Church and the state, but also the Church and the people.  Guided by the innovative Peruvian priest, Gustavo Gutiérrez, this movement reinvigorated marginalized people in Peru and throughout Latin America, while still utilizing a formal theological approach.  Though ultimately opposed by the Vatican because of its radical leanings, liberation theology both permanently implicated the Church in the destiny of the oppressed and allowed for the participation of the poor in the future of the Catholic Church.

20. what does liberal education mean according to the greeks


WHAT IS LIBERAL EDUCATION?

- Liberal Education is an approach to learning that is focused on the individual empowerment, with the agenda to prepare them in comprehending diversity, complexity, and change.

- The goal of Liberal Education is to produce individuals who are  open-minded and knowleadgable about a wide range of topics.  

- In a theoretical sense, these people are free from any form of prejudice, and are willing to openly take multiple perspectives for the sake of understanding.

- Liberal Education is often ideological. It discusses different philosophical aspects in order to analyze different forms of topics.

- Socrates was believed to be an inspiration for Liberal Education, wherein he believed that a person should be educated for them to have the ability to form their own opinion, rather than through indoctrination.

29. Meaning of Liberalism, Absolutism, Anarchism, Socialism?​


Answer:

Liberalism is a political and moral philosophy based on liberty, consent of the governed and equality before the law.

absolutism, the political doctrine and practice of unlimited centralized authority and absolute sovereignty, as vested especially in a monarch or dictator.

anarchism-belief in the abolition of all government and the organization of society on a voluntary, cooperative basis without recourse to force or compulsion.
