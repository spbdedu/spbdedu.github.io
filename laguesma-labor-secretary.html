7. what is the effects of forced labor on the filipino laborers


Forced labor is a form of work or service, in which, people are forced to do against their will, with the threat of punishment. The Philippines has a long history regarding forced labor. It all started back during the Spanish occupation of the Philippines, which was over, 497 years ago. Filipinos were subjected to forced labor, known as ''polo y Servicio''. They were given little to no compensation, a lot of Filipinos during that time became slaves, which suffered abuse and torture. At that time, it was rampant and common, Spaniards belittle the Filipinos and most of them treat our ancestors lower than animals. Forced labor was required for males from the age of 18-60, working 40 days per year, which was then reduced to 15 days by the year 1884. But, there were certain Filipinos who were exempted from the said labor. They were the aristocrats, those who could pay taxes which amounted to 70 pesos during that time. To put it simply, those who were rich and those who belong to the rich family were exempted.

Before its effectiveness, ''Polo y Servicio'' was put into writing and had various conditions along with it. These are, the labor should be done and used for necessary public projects, laborers must be fully compensated, physical condition and health of the laborers must be thoroughly checked before they are subjected to labor, laborers should work near their homes, and they should be given a week or a month off so they could harvest and plant crops. Sadly, all of these conditions were not met and they were subjected to abuse and injustice. Most of them were treated inhumanely, tortured, and even sexually assaulted. Compensations were not given and they were forced to work for free. A lot of them died due to being sick, getting overworked, from torture, or straight-up getting murdered. Others were even tasked to do dirty work for the aristocrats and those in power, especially those in the church, particularly the priests. Which squandered the farmlands of the farmers and forced them to work on their own farm, with no pay or just compensation. Some Filipinos were even forced to sell out their fellow countrymen.  

To answer the question, ''What is the effects of forced labor on the Filipino laborers?'' Forced labor lead to a chain of hatred and resentment of the Filipinos towards their colonizers, the Spaniards. They also resented those of the upper-class, including their fellow Filipinos who sided with the Spaniards. These lead to an uprising, a string of revolts that led to a wide revolution. It ignited the war towards freedom. Filipinos against Spaniards and Filipinos against fellow Filipinos who betrayed them. The war involved a lot of bloodshed from both sides, it was like a match that ignited a bush which spread like hellfire. It resulted in more Filipinos from all over the country to stand up and fight against their oppressors. The abuse that they dealt gave birth to the heroes that we know as of today.

Answer:
