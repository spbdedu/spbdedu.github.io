2. musical instrument ilocano ​


Bamboo has been the source of many musical instruments in the province: pito (flute), kulkultong (bamboo zither) and (jaws harp), among others. Other traditional instruments are the ludag (log drum) and tangguyob (carabao horn). Western-influenced instruments include the arpa (harp) and tambor (drum).

ILOCANO FOLK SONGS Pamulinawen The most popular of the old songs of music-loving Ilocanos is the “Pamulinawen. ” It is believed that it was created by an Ilocano troubadour of pre. Hispanic times who was inspired by the sweetness of an Ilocana. The maiden might have been resplendent with character traits cherished by the women of Ilocandia and for this reason the troubadour must have called her “Pamulinawen” which according to the text of the song is the sweetest and most illustrious names among Ilocanos of old.

30. 23. It is a combination ofSpanish and Ilocano dance steps and music.​


Answer:

La Jota Moncadeña

Explanation:

La Jota Moncadeña was adapted from an old Spanish dance. It is a combination of Spanish and Ilocano dance set to Spanish music and castanets. A more solemn version of the dance is sometimes used to accompany a funeral procession, but it was also performed at the festival.
