12. What are the difference between the christmas and new year rites of Philippines and America, and what are their similarities?


Christmas is a joyous occasion for thousands of people around the world. Each country has their own set of traditions and celebrations that they practice during the holidays. Since I moved to San Diego for my undergraduate studies, I noticed that there are several similarities and differences in the way Christmas is celebrated in the U.S. and in my home country, the Philippines.

Both the U.S. and the Philippines have similar Christmas traditions such as decorating the residences and public areas with Christmas decorations, putting up Christmas trees, and passing on the belief of Santa Claus. However, a lot of families in the U.S. tend to get a real spruce, pine or fir tree for their homes. Since these trees are not native to the Philippines, Filipino families tend to get artificial trees instead. Additionally, the Philippines has a special Christmas lantern called a parol. It is a star shaped lantern made of capiz shells and bamboo. Some parols are made with paper or cellophane. This lantern represents the Star of David.

Both countries also sing Christmas carols, gather for Christmas meals, and open presents on Christmas morning. It is important to note that in both countries, not everyone celebrates Christmas. There are a mix of beliefs in both countries. However, the Philippines is predominantly Catholic.

A major difference of how Christmas is celebrated in the U.S. versus in the Philippines is the food that is served during Christmastime. In the U.S., the typical food served on Christmas day consists of turkey and ham. The food also varies between families and regions in the U.S. due to its diverse set of cultures. Some families serve tamales, roast goose, jambalaya, and roast pork. In the Philippines, there is a special meal served on Christmas Eve called Noche Buena. It consists of lechon (roasted pig), ham, fruit salad, and rice cakes called bibingka and puto bumbong. Then on Christmas morning, families serve ensaymada (a type of pastry made of bread and cheese), ham and hot chocolate. Classic Filipino dishes are also served on Christmas day such as kare-kare (stew made with peanut sauce, pork, beef and tripe) and embutido (type of cured sausage).

The Philippines also has their own unique set of traditions during the festive season. As early as September, malls start selling Christmas decorations and playing Christmas music. The Christmas commercials also start playing on the TV. The Philippines also has a tradition called Simbang Gabi (Night Mass), which is a series of 4 am masses held from December 16 to 24. If one completes all nine masses, a wish they make will come true.

Despite the fact that Christmas is celebrated differently around the world, it still remains a time of giving and of spending time with loved ones. Even though not everyone celebrates Christmas, the winter holidays are a time for peace, love and happiness that can be shared with every person and every culture.

-Gia Pedro, Math – Economics, ’21

India

What Do People do?

People in India dress brightly and participate in enjoyable events such as singing, playing sports, dancing, and holding parties. People of all ages congregate in nightclubs, movie theaters, casinos, bars, and amusement parks.

People welcome one another and wish each other a Happy New Year. Messages, greeting cards, and presents are all part of the New Year's festivities. Many New Year's Eve activities are covered by the public, and they are shown on prime channels for the majority of the day. People who want to sit at home for the holidays rely on these New Year's Eve shows for entertainment and fun. The age-old ritual of making new year's resolutions is still widespread. Losing weight, cultivating healthier habits, and working hard are some of the most common resolutions.

Public Life

In India, New Year's Day, January 1 in the Gregorian calendar, is a restricted holiday. Individuals can take a small number of restricted holidays, but government departments, most companies, and public transit remain open. Owing to late-night parties, most people are known to arrive at work late on January 1.

Philippines

What Do People Do?

On New Year's Eve, December 31, many Filipinos gather to enjoy a midnight meal known as the Media Noche. It is therefore customary to remain up until the New Year arrives. Filipinos also light fireworks and make a lot of noise on New Year's Day to ward off bad spirits. The Chinese were the originators of this belief.

The elderly inspire their children to hop at the stroke of midnight in order for them to grow tall. Many people show 12 circular fruits and wear polka-dot clothing to reflect wealth. It is also customary to open all doors and windows at the stroke of midnight to welcome good luck. Many Filipino families still read the Christian bible and attend midnight worship services. Most Filipinos celebrate New Year's Day by combining faith and superstition.

Public Life

On January 1, the Philippines observes a national holiday known as New Year's Day. Government buildings, classrooms, and the majority of businesses have been closed. During New Year's Day, public transportation is limited to restricted bus and jeep services.

1. Media Noche in the Philippines

media noche filipino new year traditions  

What is Media Noche in the Philippines? It is one of the most important traditions for Filipinos because this is where family members are complete, relatives working abroad or in other provinces come home to spend time with the family. This Filipino New Years tradition symbolizes how locals value family time so much.  

During media noche in the Philippines, the families exchange gifts, share stories, and enjoy specially prepared Filipino new year handa together while waiting for the countdown to welcome the new year.  

2. Wearing polka dots

polka dots new year

This is one of the New Year traditions Philippines that’s believed to bring good luck. Whether the polka dot pattern is on your shirt, pants, or shoes, it’s important for the Filipinos to wear polka dots because round shapes are said to bring in prosperity and wealth.  

3. Jumping high at 12 midnight

two people jumping filipino new year traditions

When the clock strikes 12, Filipinos would jump as high as they can in the hopes of getting taller. Believe it or not, most kids (even adults) have done this.

4. Making loud noises. A lot of it

party horns  

A New Year’s Eve in the Philippines isn’t complete without the loud noises. While celebrating New Year anywhere in the world involves noises, we think that it’s only in the Philippines where you’ll hear people making loud noises, a lot of it, in welcoming the new year.  

Usually, they use colorful horns to make noises. But if they don’t have it, they make use of everything around them like the cooking pans, pots, and spoons and hit them like they’re drummers. Well, what do you expect from resourceful Filipinos?  

Loud noises are said to drive both evil spirits and bad luck away.

5. Preparing 12 round fruits for good luck

bilog na prutas bagong taon sa pilipinas  

If you want to experience luck the whole year, you must prepare 12 round fruits before the new year. The number of the fruits represents each month of the year while the shape is believed to bring wealth and prosperity. This is one of the Filipino New Year food traditions that’s widely practiced.

6. Eating pancit for long life

pansit malabon

 

Filipino New Year food tradition is eating pancit. Pancit has always been a staple in every occasion in the Philippines because it symbolizes long life and good health. But, some people say not to cut the noodles before you eat them or it could defeat the purpose.  

7. Eating sticky rice cakes

tikoy bagong taon sa pilipinas

 

One of the Philippines New Year traditions that never goes is preparing sticky foods like rice cakes in welcoming the new year. The most popular is tikoy ad biko. Filipinos believed that eating sticky rice will help strengthen and improve the relations and bond of the family. This is one of the oldest new year food in the Philippines.

8. Preparing whole pork

lechon

Another lucky food for New Year Philippines is whole pork. They say that the whole pork’s size symbolizes prosperity. One of the best new year food ideas in the Philippines for foodies.

9. Preparing whole grilled fish

inihaw na bangus

Preparing whole grilled fish is one of the New Year traditions Philippines that’s believed to bring good luck. The fish’s scale symbolizes coins while its way of swimming in the ocean represents a life moving forward.

10. Opening windows and doors

a girl opening windows filipino new year traditions  

In welcoming the new year, it’s a must for some Filipinos to open all the windows and doors at their house. This New Year tradition in the Philippines is practiced in the hopes that good luck will enter their home.
