9. Mario has 45 red chips, 12 blue chips, and 24 white chips. What is the Probabality that Mario randomly selects a red chips or white chips and blue chips and white chips.​


Answer:

16/243.

Step-by-step explanation:

The probability of randomly selecting a red chip or a white chip can be calculated by adding the probabilities of selecting a red chip and selecting a white chip.

P(red or white) = P(red) + P(white)

To calculate each probability, we divide the number of red chips and white chips by the total number of chips.

P(red) = 45/(45+12+24) = 45/81 = 5/9

P(white) = 24/(45+12+24) = 24/81 = 8/27

Therefore,

P(red or white) = P(red) + P(white) = 5/9 + 8/27

To add these fractions, we need to find a common denominator, which is 27 x 9 = 243.

P(red or white) = (5/9 x 27/27) + (8/27 x 9/9)

P(red or white) = 135/243 + 72/243

P(red or white) = 207/243

The probability of randomly selecting a red chip or a white chip is 207/243.

Now, let's calculate the probability of randomly selecting a blue chip and a white chip.

P(blue and white) = P(blue) x P(white)

To calculate each probability, we divide the number of blue chips and white chips by the total number of chips.

P(blue) = 12/(45+12+24) = 12/81 = 4/27

P(white) = 24/(45+12+24) = 24/81 = 8/27

Therefore,

P(blue and white) = P(blue) x P(white) = 4/27 x 8/27

P(blue and white) = 32/729

The probability of randomly selecting a blue chip and a white chip is 32/729.

So, the probability of randomly selecting a red chip or white chip and a blue chip and a white chip is:

P(red or white) x P(blue and white) = (207/243) x (32/729)

P(red or white and blue and white) = 16/243

Therefore, the probability of Mario randomly selecting a red chip or white chip and a blue chip and white chip is 16/243.
