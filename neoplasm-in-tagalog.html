Morbidity refers to the state of being sick or sickening, as well as the level of sickness in a community. Morbidity also refers to the medical issues that arise as a result of therapy. The morbidity rate examines how many people in a certain population, at a specific geographic region, contracted a specific disease over a specified time period. Morbidity is a metric that assesses the likelihood of a person developing a debilitating ailment that limits their capacity to operate independently. With one or more morbidities, a person can live for several years, and one morbidity can progress to another. brainly.com/question/26202827

What is mortality?

The death rate in a population is known as the mortality rate. Mortality is calculated by multiplying the number of deaths per thousand people by the child mortality rate, crude death rates, newborn mortality, maternal mortality, and so on. brainly.ph/question/813558
