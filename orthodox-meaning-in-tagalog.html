12. what is the difference between Catholic and Orthodox?​


Answer:

The Catholic Church and the Orthodox Church are two of the largest Christian denominations in the world. While they share many beliefs and practices, there are also some significant differences between the two churches.

One of the main differences between the Catholic Church and the Orthodox Church is their structure and organization. The Catholic Church is a centralized organization with a single leader, the Pope, who has authority over all Catholics worldwide. The Orthodox Church, on the other hand, is a decentralized organization with multiple leaders, known as patriarchs or primates, who have authority over different regions or jurisdictions.

Another significant difference between the two churches is their liturgical practices and traditions. The Catholic Church follows the Latin Rite, which includes the use of Latin in the Mass and other liturgical ceremonies, and a more formalized liturgical structure. The Orthodox Church follows various liturgical traditions, such as the Byzantine Rite, which includes the use of local languages and a more flexible liturgical structure.

There are also some differences in the beliefs and practices of the two churches. For example, the Catholic Church believes in the concept of Purgatory, while the Orthodox Church does not. The Orthodox Church also has a different understanding of the role of Mary, the mother of Jesus, and the authority of the Pope.

Despite these differences, the Catholic Church and the Orthodox Church share many beliefs and practices, and both are committed to spreading the message of Jesus Christ and serving the needs of their communities.
