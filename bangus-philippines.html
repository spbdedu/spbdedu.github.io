1. Why is bangus the national fish of the philippines?​


Answer:

Bangus is the national fish of Philippines, called milkfish in English. It is an adaptable, tough, and sturdy fish that can survive in confined spaces, so it is regularly bred on fish farms, which is the reason why it is widely available all over the Philippines.

13. what is the significant of the following symbols? ;1. national emblem 2. national hero (Jose Rizal)3. National Flag (Philippine Flag)4. National Fish (Bangus)​


significances of the following symbols:

National emblem - The national emblem of the Philippines features a golden eagle, a Philippine eagle, and a shield. The eagle represents strength and freedom, while the shield symbolizes the nation's defense. This emblem serves as the official seal of the President of the Philippines and is used in official government documents.

National hero (Jose Rizal) - Jose Rizal is considered the national hero of the Philippines because of his role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonialism in the late 19th century. He was a writer, philosopher, and polymath who used his talents to advocate for reforms and inspire his fellow Filipinos to fight for their rights.

National Flag (Philippine Flag) - The Philippine Flag is a symbol of the country's sovereignty and independence. It features two colors: blue and red, which represent peace and courage, respectively. The white triangle on the left side of the flag symbolizes equality, while the eight rays of the sun represent the eight provinces that first revolted against Spanish colonialism.

National Fish (Bangus) - Bangus, also known as milkfish, is considered the national fish of the Philippines. It is a popular and important part of the country's cuisine, and it is also seen as a symbol of the Filipino people's resilience and adaptability. Bangus can survive in both freshwater and saltwater, and it has become a staple food for many Filipinos because of its versatility and taste.
