16. What is suman description?​


Answer:

Suman is a rice cake originating in the Philippines. It is made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk, often wrapped in banana leaves, coconut leaves, or buli or buri palm (Corypha) leaves for steaming. It is usually eaten sprinkled with sugar or laden with latik.

25. Why kakanin is called kakanin? ​


Answer:

The name kakanin is derived from two Tagalog words: “kain” (to eat) and “kanin” (rice).

Explanation:

It's an umbrella term for sweets made of glutinous rice and coconut milk, two ingredients that tropical countries like ours have in abundance. These ingredients are usually employed in one of two forms.
