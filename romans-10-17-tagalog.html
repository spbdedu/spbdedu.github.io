17. Lesson # 1 1. The Word of God is active in our salvation. Briefly explain how the following verses apply to this. a. 1 Peter 1:23 b. Romans 10:17 C. James 1:21​


Explanation:

a. 1 Peter 1:23 - This verse states that we are born again not by corruptible seed, but by the incorruptible Word of God which lives and abides forever. It implies that the Word of God has the power to bring spiritual life and salvation to those who believe.

b. Romans 10:17 - This verse declares that faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God. It emphasizes the importance of hearing and believing the Word of God as a means to obtain salvation.

c. James 1:21 - This verse instructs us to receive with meekness the engrafted Word which is able to save our souls. It highlights the need to not only hear, but also receive and apply the Word of God in our lives for our salvation.

20. Reflection about Romans 10:13-15​


Background:

Romans 10, as a whole, talks that salvation comes through righteousness to those who believe in Christ and that faith comes by hearing the gospel taught by legal administrators sent of God.

Verses 13-15 specifically implies the importance of preachers "sent" by the Lord to teach and preach unto the people who do not believe. If they accept, they will also be saved.

Reflection:

If we want to be saved in the last day, we should believe in Christ and obey his will. If He calls us to preach, then we must go and do His command.
