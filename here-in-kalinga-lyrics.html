10. Answer the following.1. The last mambabatok in kalinga2. Another form of art of the Kalinga3. The wrap-round skirt of the women in Kalinga4. The major weaving center in Kalinga5. The traditional color combination used in Kalinga's weaving ​


Answer:

1. Whang-od Oggay is known as the last mambabatok (Kalinga tattooist) from the tribe of Butbut in Buscalan, Kalinga. She has been tattooing for the past 80 years — including head hunters of the indigenous tribe, at the beginning of her long career.

2. Tattooing is another form of art of the Kalinga. Because it usually covers the chest and the arms, it may be considered as clothing and decoration.

3. Kalinga clothing is particularly brightly coloured and is often dominated by bands of fiery red. This type of wraparound skirt is generally known as tapis in the Tagalog language and as kain to the Kalinga.

4. The major weaving center in Kalinga is the village of Mabilong in the town of Lubuagan. The weavers of Mabilong are known for their backstrap weaving techniques, which they pass on to their daughters as soon as they are able to operate the loom.

5. Kalinga weaves are characterized by the traditional color combinations of red and black stripes and the use of beads.

15. Emphasis of kalinga​


Answer:

Emphasis Kalinga

Explanation:

Kalinga is both a tribal community and a landlocked province in the heart of the Cordillera Region, North Luzon, the Philippines. Until recently Kalinga people could be identified from a distance by their distinctive body art. Immersed in the magnificent mountains and cut off from modern society, Kalinga people lived modest but passionate lives in a world where your skin communicated your social status to the local community.

In February  2016 I travelled to the Kalinga capitol of Tabuk where I was a guest at the annual Kalinga Foundation Day festival. There I was able to interview Lasoy, one of the few surviving members of her generation with compulsory Kalinga tattoos, on what it meant to be a Kalinga woman, and her personal journey of both love and loathing for her skin art.


16. what is the differences of kalinga tattoo kalinga and jewerly​


Answer:

The ink used for Kalinga tattoo is made from indigenous materials. Inside a coconut shell is charcoal mixed with water inside pierced into the skin with a sharp thorn needle from a calamansi tree. The first bamboo stick is used for holding the thorn in place, the other is used to tap the first to pierce the skin.
