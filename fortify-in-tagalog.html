Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

A one-tailed test is a statistical test in which the critical area of a distribution is one-sided so that it is either greater than or less than a certain value, but not both. If the sample being tested falls into the one-sided critical area, the alternative hypothesis will be accepted instead of the null hypothesis.

Since the nutritionist directly claims the supposed nutrient of her developed bread, it falls as one sided. Since it is a statistical hypothesis shows that the sample mean would be higher or lower of the sample mean, but not both.

30. Give at least three examples of fortified products and convinience food.​


Answer: Here are three examples of fortified products and convenience food:

Explanation:

1. Fortified Cereals: Many breakfast kinds of cereal are fortified with vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and B vitamins. These nutrients are added to the cereal during the manufacturing process to provide consumers with a convenient and easy way to get essential nutrients in their diet.

2. Instant Noodles: Instant noodles are a popular convenience food that is easy and quick to prepare. They are often fortified with vitamins and minerals, such as iron, B vitamins, and calcium, to increase their nutritional value.

3. Energy Bars: Energy bars are a popular snack for people on the go. They are fortified with vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin D, calcium, and iron, to provide a convenient source of nutrients for people who may not have time for a full meal. Some energy bars also contain added protein and fiber to help consumers stay full and energized throughout the day.
