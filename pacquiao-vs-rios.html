13. Rio and Kim had the same amount of money at the start. When Rio gave PHP300 to Kim,the ratio of Rio's money to Kims money became 2:3. How much money did each have at first?


Let Rio's money be X, Kim's money be Y.

First situation: 
X=Y

When Php 300 was given;
X-300 and Y+300.

Since the ratio is 2:3,
3(X-300)=2(Y+300)
3X-900=2Y+600
3X-2Y= 600+900

Since X=Y,

3X-2X= X= 1500 

or,

3Y-2Y= Y= 1500.

∴ they both had Php 1500 each at first.

29. Rio and Kim had the same amount of money at the start. When Rio gave Kim 300 pesos to Kim. The ratio of Rio's money to Kim's is 2:3. How much money did they have at the start?


Original amount of money:

Rio: x
Kim: x

New amount of money with the ratio of 2:3.

Rio: x - 300   (gave 300 to Kim)
Kim: x + 300 (add 300 the amount from Rio)

Solution:

(x - 300) : (x + 300) = 2:3

Equate the product of means to the product of extremes:

2 (x+300) = 3 (x - 300)
2x + 600 = 3x - 900
3x - 2x = 600 + 900
x = 1,500

ANSWER: The original amount of money each has is 1,500 pesos.

Check:

Rio's money to Kim's money = 2:3

(1,500-300) / (1,500 + 300) = 2/3
1,200 / 1,800 = 2/3

Divide the numerator 1,200 and denominator 1,800 by their GCF 600 to simplify to the lowest term:

(1,200/600) / (1,800/600) = 2/3
2/3 = 2/3  (True)
