22. where did the first pampanga revolt happen?​


Answer:

Pampanga

Explanation:

The First Pampanga Revolt in 1585 was undertaken by native Kapampangan leaders against Spanish encomienderos due to abuses felt by the natives inflicted by the encomienderos. The revolt included a plot to storm Intramuros. However, the plot was foiled before it was even implemented, since a Filipina married to a Spanish soldier reported the plot to Spanish authorities. For their actions, the leaders of the revolt were ordered executed.

Answer:

The first Pampanga revolt occurred in the Philippines in 1889. It was a rebellion against Spanish colonial rule led by a group of revolutionary leaders from the province of Pampanga. The revolt was sparked by a number of grievances, including widespread poverty, social injustices, and harsh taxes. The rebels were ultimately unsuccessful in their efforts to overthrow the Spanish colonial government, and many of the leaders of the revolt were captured and executed. However, the Pampanga revolt served as an important precedent for the Philippine Revolution that would take place a decade later, and contributed to the eventual independence of the Philippines from Spanish rule.
