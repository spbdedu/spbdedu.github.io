Answer:

Florante at Laura (full title: Pinagdaanang Buhay ni Florante at ni Laura sa Kahariang Albanya; English: The History of Florante and Laura in the Kingdom of Albania) is an 1838 awit written by Tagalog poet Francisco Balagtas. It is considered one of the masterpieces of Philippine literature. Balagtas wrote the epic during his imprisonment.[2] He dedicated it to his former sweetheart María Asuncion Rivera, whom he nicknamed "M.A.R." and is referenced to as "Selya" in the dedication "Kay Selya" ("For Celia"). The story is loosely based on his own biography.

About Literary works

Literary works are creations that communicate the author's intent for aesthetic purposes. There are different forms of literature.

There are several functions of literature, one of which is to convey ideas and convey the thoughts and feelings of the human aesthetic creator.

Literature is not only an art of language, but also a skill in using language with literary form and value.

Types of literary works

•Poetry

Poetry is a type of literature whose forms are carefully chosen and arranged. It sharpens people's awareness of experiences and generates specific responses through special sounds, rhythms, and meanings.

• Prose (fiction)

Prose is one form of literary work that differs from poetry. However, many think that literary works are the same.

Prose is a literary work organized in the form of a story or narrative. Prose is usually a hybrid of monologue form with dialogue.

• novel

The novel is a form of literary work in the form of prose, which has intrinsic and extrinsic elements. The word novel comes from the Italian word 'novella' which means story or story.

• Romance

A romance is a long story about heroism and love. The term romance developed in Germany, the Netherlands, France, and other parts of mainland Europe.

There is a slight difference between the romance and the novel, especially the novel form is shorter than the romance, but the size of the broad elements of the story is almost the same.

• Short story

The short story (short story) is a form of literary work in narrative form that is completely fictional. It should not exceed 10,000 words.

The short story or often abbreviated as the short story is a form of fictional narrative prose. Short stories tend to be dense and to the point compared to longer works of fiction.

• Drama

Drama is a type of literature in the form of poetry or prose that aims to describe life through the behavior and dialogue of characters. Generally, plays are designed for stage performances.

GENRE OF PHILIPPINE LITERATURE

1. To my Fellow Youth (Sa Aking Mga Kabata) by: Dr. Jose P. Rizal

Genre: Poetic verses /Poetry

Poetry Genre: Lyrical Poetry

REASON: Rizal's resolves to relay his love for the country by asking the youth to do the same. A formal, typically decorous lyric poetry in which a person, place, thing, or concept is addressed and often celebrated. Its stanza shapes change.

2. The Crow and the Sparrow

Genre: Fable

Reason: Fable is a genre of a story where the characters are animals but emulates characteristics of humans. Like they are talking or having thoughts and even personalities.  The main characters, Crow and the Sparrow, are talking and having characteristics similar to humans.

3.  The Origin of Sampaguita

Genre: Legend

Reason: A legend is a traditional historical narrative. The Origin of Sampaguita (Alamat ng Sampaguita) is a legend to tell kids about its history before it existed. It is meant to convey a moral of the story.

4.  The Prodigal Son

Genre: Parable

Reason: A fictional narrative that expresses a moral attitude or a religious idea.

5. The Rat and the Lion

Genre: Fable

Reason: The story of animals acting like people to convey the story of friendship.

6. The Philippines During Martial Law

Genre: Non-Fiction

Non-Fiction Genre: Historical or Academic Texts

Reason: The book details a part of history in the Philippines and are written using data, photographs, writings, news and verified sources.

7. Florante and Laura

Genre: Fiction /Epic Poetry

Reason: An epic is a large, sometimes book-length, verse-based tale that recounts the heroic adventure of an individual or a group of people. Epics are distinguished by superhuman feats, fantastic experiences, stylized language, and a merging of poetic and theatrical elements.

8. The Battle of Tirrad Pass

Genre: Non-Fiction /Historical Anecdote

Reason: This historical context was about Gregorio Del Pilar's tactic to ambush the Americans. Gregorio del Pilar is a real person who died in the Filipino-American War.
