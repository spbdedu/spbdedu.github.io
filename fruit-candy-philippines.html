1. rita bought a bag of milk and fruit candies weighing 268 grams. The milk candies weigh 4 grams each and the fruit candies weigh 5 grams each. If there are 32 milk candies, how many fruit candies are there in the bag?


First, you multiply the number of milk candies to its weight.
32 X 4 = 128.
Next, you have to subtract the result from the total weight of both candies.
268 - 128 = 140
Lastly, divide the result (140) into the weight of the fruit candies.
140/5 = 28
The answer is there are 28 fruit candies in the bag.x = number of fruit candies
5x = total weight of fruit candies
(4) (32) or 128 = total weight of milk candies
268 = total number of fruit and milk candies combined.

Equation:
Weight of milk candies + weight of fruit candies = 268

128 + 5x = 268
5x = 268 - 128
5x = 140
5x/5 = 140/5
x = 28

ANSWER:  There are 28 fruit candies.

17. Bob bought sme fruit candies weighing 5 grams each and some milk candies weighing 4 grams each. If he has 100 candies altogether and they weigh 480 grams in total, how many of each kind does he have?


Let:
x = no. of fruit candies
y = no. of milk candies

x + y = 100
y = 100 - x --> Eq1

5x + 4y = 480 ( substitute y from Eq1)
5x + 4(100 - x) = 480
5x + 400 - 4x = 480
x = 480 - 400
x = 80

Solve for y ( using Eq1)
y = 100 - x
y = 100 - 80
y = 20

Answer:
Bob has 80 fruit candies and 20 milk candies
