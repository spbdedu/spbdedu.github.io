Answer:

1. Technique: False Dilemma/ Either-Or Fallacy

Explanation: The example presents a false dilemma by implying that one must choose between milk tea or fruit juice, and that milk tea is the better choice for the current generation.

2. Technique: Glittering Generalities

Explanation: The example uses positive words such as "beautiful" and "young" without providing any specific information or evidence about the product being promoted.

3. Technique: Bandwagon Appeal

Explanation: The example appeals to the desire to be part of a group or trend by implying that everyone is choosing the Everything Store and suggesting that the viewer should too.

4. Technique: Transfer

Explanation: The example uses patriotic images and language to suggest that the product is of high quality because it is made in the Philippines, without providing any actual evidence.

5. Technique: Fear-mongering

Explanation: The example uses scare tactics by implying that not using the SoftyTouch beauty product will result in forever dry skin
