14. When one dominant allele is absent, only a recessive allele will combine with another recessive allele. the absence of dominant alleles follow a pattern of inheritance called?


Answer:

If the two alleles are the same, the individual is homozygous for that allele. If the alleles are different, the individual is heterozygous.

Individuals who have two identical alleles for a given gene, whether dominant or recessive, are said to be homozygous for that gene (homo- = “same”). Conversely, an individual who has one dominant allele and one recessive allele is said to be heterozygous for that gene (hetero- = “different” or “other”).
