30. Will you be able to collect dna only on living things or once living? Why?


Answer:

DNA is found in nearly all living cells. ... In short, DNA is a complex molecule that consists of many components, a portion of which are passed from parent organisms to their offspring during the process of reproduction. Although each organism's DNA is unique, all DNA is composed of the same nitrogen-based molecules. And yes, you can still be able to collect DNA even of that person/organism is already dead.
