4. logal name of carabao grass​


Answer:

paspalum conjugatum

Explanation:

paspalum conjugatum commonly know as carabao grass or hilo grass

Paspalum conjugatum

Paspalum conjugatum, commonly known as carabao grass or hilo grass, is a tropical to subtropical perennial grass. It is originally from the American tropics, but has been naturalized widely in tropical Southeast Asia and Pacific Islands. It has also spread to Northern Africa and Northern and Eastern Australia. It is also known as sour paspalum, T-grass (after the shape of their panicle), or more confusingly, as "buffalo grass" or "sour grass".

16. how to prolong the shelf life of carabao mango​


Answer:

The shelf-life of carabao mango fruits could be effectively prolonged by about 4 to 8 days by treating them with NAA 200 ppm and packing in polythene or polythene + KMnO4.

Explanation:

Answer:

Mango is one of the country's flagship commodities for export. To boost the industry, fresh mangoes should undergo pre- and postharvest disease management protocols to extend shelf life and enable long distance transport to export markets. CA storage is a technology that extends the shelf life of various perishable products. Previous studies by Filipino scientists generated protocols for Philippine 'Carabao' mangoes were conducted using CA storage data or protocols. To complement earlier results, Cuaresma et al. (CLSU) conducted a stationary trial of Philippine 'Carabao' mango using actual CA container vans or reefers equipped with CA systems at the Diamond Star Agro Product, Inc. (DSAPI), FTI Complex, Taguig, Metro Manila [Philippines]. The study was conducted from October 2005 to June 2006. Findings of the study revealed the following: CA storage with present levels of 6% CO2, 4% O2 and pulp of 13 deg C temperature delays the ripening process and extended shelf life to 29 days. With CA and at pulp temperature of 13 deg C, the ripening process was delayed for 21 days. Mangoes stored under CA for 28 days at the preset conditions were comparable in terms of characteristics and quality with mango stored under non-CA conditions at preset pulp temperature of 13 deg C for 21 days.
