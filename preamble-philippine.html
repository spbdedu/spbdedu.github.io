THE 1987 CONSTITUTION OF THE REPUBLIC OF THE PHILIPPINES – PREAMBLE

PREMBLE

We, the sovereign Filipino people, imploring the aid of Almighty God, in order to build a just and humane society, and establish a Government that shall embody our ideals and aspirations, promote the common good, conserve and develop our patrimony, and secure to ourselves and our posterity, the blessings of independence and democracy under the rule of law and a regime of truth, justice, freedom, love, equality, and peace, do ordain and promulgate this Constitution.

The republic had a constitution drafted by Isabelo Artacho and Félix Ferrer and was based on the first Cuban Constitution. It is known as the "Constitución Provisional de la República de Filipinas", and was originally written in and promulgated in the Spanish and Tagalog languages. The preamble sets the stage for the Constitution. It clearly communicates the intentions of the framers and the purpose of the document. The preamble is an introduction to the highest law of the land; it is not the law. It does not define government powers or individual rights.

Filipino values ​​presented in the preamble These are: Unity, Patriotism, Faith in the Almighty God, work, Respect for Life, Respect for Law and Government, Truth, Justice, Freedom, Love, Equality, Peace, Promotion of the Common Good, Concern for the Family and future Generations, Order, and Concern for the Environment. Underlying Principles.

26. Role and importance of Preamble of the Philippines​.


Answer:

The preamble of the Philippines is a statement that introduces and describes the fundamental principles and values that underlie the constitution of the Philippines. It sets out the purposes and goals of the constitution, and serves as a guide for the interpretation and application of the constitution.

The preamble of the Philippines begins with the phrase "We, the sovereign Filipino people," which acknowledges the sovereignty of the people and the role they play in the creation and maintenance of the constitution. It then goes on to declare the values and principles that the constitution is based on, including:

The promotion of the common good and the general welfare of the peopleThe protection of the rights and freedoms of the peopleThe maintenance of peace and order, and the strengthening of national defenseThe fostering of unity and cooperation among all the regions and cultural communities of the PhilippinesThe promotion of social justice and the reduction of social, economic, and political inequities

The preamble of the Philippines is an important part of the constitution because it sets out the fundamental principles and values that guide the actions and policies of the government and the people of the Philippines. It serves as a reference point for interpreting the constitution and for determining the constitutionality of laws and actions. It also serves as a rallying point for the people, reminding them of their shared values and aspirations as a nation.
