2. nakakahawang sakit english


Nakakahawa in English is contagious or infectious.

Sakit in English is sickness or disease.

Combining the two words together, the English equivalent of nakakahawang sakit is infectious disease.

In the field of science and technology, infectious diseases are caused by pathogens or germs (e.g. bacteria, viruses) which can easily invade the human immune system because of its microscopic size.

These germs cause people to become sick. Once they grew in the body of the host, such pathogens can be transmitted through oral or air which also causes other people to catch the illness.

Such infectious diseases can cause epidemics and kill large numbers of people.

25. Essay about the saying sakit ng kalingkingan dama ng buong katawan in English


Answer:

“Ang sakit ng kalingkingan ay ramdam ng buong katawan.” Meaning, it is a good sign when we are able to feel the pain of the other parts of the Body of Christ. What is bad is when we don’t feel it at all, when we remain indifferent about it. When Christians behave this way, it could only mean that the body is sick, that some parts are probably numb or festering with gangrene.”
