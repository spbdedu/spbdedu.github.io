15. Who was the senate president of the philippines when r.a. 9163 was passed?


President Gloria Macapagal- Arroyo was the senate president of the Philippines when r.a. 9163 was passed

Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo was the second female president of the Republic of the Philippines, serving from 2001 to 2009. In addition, she was the nation's first female vice president.

Arroyo, who obtained a master's and Ph.D. in economics, centered her administration on the Philippine economy. During the administration of President Arroyo, the Philippines' annual economic growth averaged 4.5% and grew every quarter. This is greater than her three immediate predecessors Corazon Aquino (3.8%), Fidel Ramos (3.7%), and Joseph Estrada (3.7%). [32] In 2007, the Philippine economy increased at its strongest rate in three decades, with real GDP expansion reaching seven percent.

Due to minimal exposure to troubled international securities, lower dependence on exports, relatively resilient domestic consumption, large remittances from four to five million overseas Filipino workers, and a growing business process outsourcing industry, the economy was one of the few to avoid contraction during the 2008 global financial crisis. Former U.S. President Bill Clinton praised Arroyo's handling of the economy, citing her "tough decisions" that put the Philippine economy back in order. Despite this rise, the poverty rate remained unchanged as a result of rapid population expansion and unequal income distribution.

Early in her presidency, Arroyo implemented a contentious strategy of holiday economics, changing holidays to create longer weekends in order to increase domestic tourism and give Filipinos more time to spend with their family.
