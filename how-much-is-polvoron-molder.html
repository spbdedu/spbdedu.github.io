8. how much larger is a leche flan made in 12 inch square molder than a leche flan made in 12 inch diameter circular molder?​


In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression and basic mathematics especially in solving word problem about area.

Problem

How much larger is a leche flan made in 12 inch square molder than a leche flan made in 12 inch diameter circular molder?

Given

s (square) = 12 inch

d (circle) = 12 inch

Solution

Solve for the area of two molders.

Area of square = s²

Area of square = 12²

Area of square = 144 in²

Area of circle = 1/4πd²

Area of circle = 1/4π(12²)

Area of circle = 113.10 in²

Solve for Area Difference

Area of Square - Area of Circle

144 - 113.10 = 30.90 in²

Answer

The square molder is 30.90 square meter greater than circular molder.

17. Ana spends Php500.00 to start a small business selling polvoron. Each polvoron costs her Php5.00 to package. she sells each polvoron for Php6.00.1.)How much profit will she make if she sells 1,000 polvoron?2.)Write a polynomial model for the profit she makes selling x polvoron.​


1. In general, profit is calculated as Profit = Total Sales – Total Expenses.

Total Sales = 1, 000 × 6 = 6, 000

Total Expenses = 1, 000 × 5 + 500 = 5, 000 + 500 = 5, 500

Profit = 6, 000 – 5, 500 = 500

Answer : 500

2. In mathematics, a polynomial is an expression consisting of variables and coefficients, that involves only the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and non-negative integer exponentiation of variables.

A polynomial in a single variable x can always be written in the form:

[tex]f(x)=a_{n} {x}^{n}+a_{n-1} {x}^{n-1}+...+ a_{2}{x}^{2}+a_{1}x+a{0}[/tex]

If Anna sells x polvoron, her sales will be Total Sales = 6 × x = 6x

Expenses will be: Total Expenses = 5 × x + 500 = 5x + 500

Profit = 6x – (5x + 500) = 6x – 5x – 500 = x – 500

Answer: x – 500

30. how to make polvoron​


Answer:

First, prepare the polvorón mold. Make sure it’s clean and dry.

Toast the flour in an oil-free pan until lightly browned and nutty in aroma. Cool completely. Otherwise, the milk and sugar will melt if mixed with the hot flour and you’ll end up with a pasty mixture. So, cool the flour.

When cool, sift together the flour, milk, sugar and salt into a bowl. Pour in the melted butter and mix until well-blended.

Fill the mold with the mixture. Use the back of a spoon to press the mixture into the mold to make sure it is firmly packed.

Push the mold handle to release the polvorón. Repeat for the rest of the mixture.
