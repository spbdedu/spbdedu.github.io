24. Which is NOT a factor in the formation of a typhoon?

Answer: C. warm ocean water

Typhoons develop from tropical thunderstorms. Strong winds draw moisture from the oceans which means it has high humidity. So letter a: high humidity is one of the factors in the formation of a typhoon. The moisture in the air is converted into heat by thunderstorms. Because of the heat, more air flows to the center of the storm, causing evaporation. Thus, giving warm river water. This is still one of the factors. The typhoon is formed when all of the heat and airflow toward the eye so letter d: optimal location of the typhoon is still not the answer because it's one of the factors in the formation of a typhoon.

25. Where do typhoons that hit the Philippines mostly originate?

Answer: A. Pacific Ocean

It is due to the Philippines' geographical location. Typhoons typically form in warm water, such as the Pacific Ocean. Most typhoons will approach the Philippines from the Pacific Ocean to the east.

26. Which set of weather conditions characterizes the eye of a typhoon?

Answer: 26. C. light winds, large clouds, heavy rains, and low temperatures

As the typhoon's eye approaches, the weather will deteriorate rapidly, with winds increasing to their strongest from the north. The weather will suddenly improve with light winds, indicating that the area is under the eye of the typhoon.

27. Which statement does NOT describe the windward side of a mountain?

Answer: 27. D. It has less vegetation because the moist in the air is accumulated in the leeward side.

The windward side is exposed to the wind and typically receives warm, moist air from the ocean. The wind is forced upward and begins to cool when it hits a mountain.

Using advances in technology, the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration or PAG-ASA provide adequate, up-to-date data and timely information on atmospheric, astronomical, and other weather-related phenomena.
