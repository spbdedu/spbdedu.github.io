Step-by-step explanation:

In some instances, a change in one quantity means a change also in another quantity. It can be a direct or inverse proportion. In direct proportion, when one quantity increases the other quantity increases, and in the same way, if one quantity decreases, the other quantity also decreases. One example is in buying goods, the more goods we buy, the higher the cost. On the other hand, in inverse proportion, when one quantity increases, the other quantity decreases, and vice versa. For example, is the speed of the object. As the speed increases, the lesser time it will take to cover a distance.
