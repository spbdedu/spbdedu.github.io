6. how is nitrogen returned to the atmosphere to complete the nitrogen cycle? ​


Answer:

Nitrogen is returned to the atmosphere by the activity of organisms known as decomposers. Some bacteria are decomposers and break down the complex nitrogen compounds in dead organisms and animal wastes. This returns simple nitrogen compounds to the soil where they can be used by plants to produce more nitrates.

Answer:

Nitrogen is returned to the atmosphere by the activity of organisms, also known as the decomposers. Some organisms or decomposers breaks down the complex nitrogen compounds in dead organisms and animal wastes. This activity returns nitrogen to the soil where they can be used to produce more nitrates.
