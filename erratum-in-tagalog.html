1. A Latin word which means error in writing or printing -ERRATUM


Erratum

Erratum meaning mistake in a printed document. The plural form of erratum is "Errata".  

The word erratum may not be a commonly used word; however, you may find this word to be used in occupations that require a higher level of writing.

Examples of how the word erratum may be used:

The book company issued an erratum to their customers when they spotted a lot of typos. An erratum at the website shows the correction for spelling errors. After the editor of the journal issued an erratum, the journal was then published by the book company.

17. A thousand years Select one: a. Millennium b. coup d' etat c. colossus d. erratum e. Habeas corpus


The answer is letter A. Millennium.

Millennium is also called kiloyears that is equal to 1000 years. Often related to a particular dating system, but not always. From the Latin word mille, meaning thousand, and annum/annus meaning year, or ennium, period of years.

Coup d’état is an illegal and violent overthrow of an existing government by a small group. Overt seizure of a state is made by military or other elite forces within the state apparatus. It is also simply called Coup. In French the word literally means a “stroke of sate” or “blow against the state” In French, the word État, denoting a sovereign political entity, is capitalized.

Colossus describes a person or thing with great size or power. Using this word to describe someone or something means they are extremely important and great in ability or size. It is from a Latin word colossus, and from ancient Greek, kolossos, meaning “large statue”.

Erratum is a mistake or error in a published printed or written text.

Habeas Corpus is a common-law writs issued to bring a person before a court or judge first before confinement or kept in a prison. It also decides whether it is legal for them to be kept in prison. It is a Medieval Latin word literally means, “that you have the body”.
