14. What happens to the oxygen-carrying capacity of hemoglobin when the pH is lowered? What is the name of this effect?


Answer:

The increase in carbon dioxide and subsequent decrease in pH reduce the affinity of hemoglobin for oxygen. The oxygen dissociates from the Hb molecule, shifting the oxygen dissociation curve to the right. Therefore, more oxygen is needed to reach the same hemoglobin saturation level as when the pH was higher.

19. name some other natural indicators that can be used to test the pH value of a substance.​


Answer:

Beets: A very basic solution (high pH) will change the color of beets or beet juice from red to purple.

Blackberries: Blackberries, black currants, and black raspberries change from red in an acidic environment to blue or violet in a basic environment.

Blueberries: Blueberries are blue around pH 2.8-3.2, but turn red as the solution becomes even more acidic.

Cherries: Cherries and their juice are red in an acidic solution, but they turn blue to purple in a basic solution.

Curry Powder: Curry contains the pigment curcumin, which changes from yellow at pH 7.4 to red at pH 8.6.

Delphinium Petals: The anthocyanin delphinidin changes from bluish-red in an acidic solution to violet-blue in a basic solution.

Geranium Petals: Geraniums contain the anthocyanin pelargonidin, which changes from orange-red in an acidic solution to blue in a basic solution.

Grapes: Red and purple grapes contain multiple anthocyanins. Blue grapes contain a monoglucoside of malvidin, which changes from deep red in an acidic solution to violet in a basic solution.

Horse Chestnut Leaves: Soak horse chestnut leaves in alcohol to extract the fluorescent dye esculin. Esculin is colorless at pH 1.5 but becomes fluorescent blue at pH 2. Get the best effect by shining a black light on the indicator.

SODIUM SULFATE with ph 7.

   Salts are formed when an acid reacts with a base during a neutralization reaction.

When a strong acid and a weak base reacts, it leads to the formation of acidic salt.When a strong base and weak acid reacts, it leads to the formation of basic salt.When a strong acid and strong base or weak acid and weak base reacts, it leads to the formation of neutral salts.    

Sodium sulfate is a neutral salt, it creates aqueous solutions with a pH of 7. The neutrality of such solutions is due to the fact that Na2SO4 is produced from a strong acid (sulfuric acid) and a strong base (sodium hydroxide).

Hence, a neutral salt and water is formed as products.

  Acidic salts are those which are formed by combination of strong acid and a weak base and have acidic pH of less than 7.

Basic salts are those which are formed by combination of weak acid and a strong base and have basic pH of more than 7.

Neutral salts are those which are formed by combination of weak acid and a weak base or by combination of strong acid and a strong base and have neutral pH equal to 7.

This leaves the majority of the population struggling to make ends meet and inhibits their ability to contribute to the economy. Without a more equitable distribution of wealth, the country cannot achieve sustainable economic growth.

Another issue is the lack of infrastructure. The Philippines has a relatively low level of infrastructure development compared to its peers in the region. Poor infrastructure has a significant impact on the country's ability to attract investment and grow its economy. Inefficient transportation systems and inadequate power supply can hamper productivity, making it difficult for businesses to operate effectively.

The Philippines also struggles with corruption. According to the Corruption Perceptions Index, the country ranks 115 out of 180 countries, indicating a high level of corruption. Corruption undermines the rule of law and makes it difficult for businesses to operate effectively. It also discourages foreign investment and leads to a loss of public trust in government institutions.

The country's education system also needs to be improved. According to the Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA), the Philippines ranks 78 out of 79 countries in terms of reading comprehension, mathematics, and science. This indicates a severe lack of investment in education and a failure to provide quality education to the majority of the population. A lack of education inhibits economic growth and development, as it limits the potential of the workforce and the ability to create a competitive and innovative business environment.

Finally, the Philippines is vulnerable to external shocks. The country heavily relies on remittances from overseas Filipino workers, which make up around 10% of the country's GDP. The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on the global economy, including the economies of countries where many Filipinos work. This has led to a decline in remittances, which has had a severe impact on the Philippines' economy.

In conclusion, the Philippines faces significant challenges that need to be addressed for the country to achieve sustainable economic growth. Income inequality, lack of infrastructure, corruption, poor education, and vulnerability to external shocks are all major issues that need to be addressed. To move forward, the country needs to face the truth behind its current economic state and implement policies that address these challenges.
