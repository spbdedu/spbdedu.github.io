4. What is the greek and roman name of mars


ROMAN NAME OF MARS

Planet Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun next to Earth, named after the war roman god Mars (Ares in Greek Mythology), it is one of the inner planets and terrestrial planets (planets with metallic and rocky composition) along with Mercury, Venus and Earth.

RED PLANET

• Mars is known as the Red Planet because of the reddish dust or abundance of iron oxide (rust) within its atmosphere or scattered over its surface. The landscape of Mars is scarred by craters, deep chasms and old volcanoes.

• Mars is the only planet along with Earth to have an atmosphere and daytime temperatures remotely akin to our planet, but the atmosphere is so thin that night temperatures plummet down to minus 130°C.
