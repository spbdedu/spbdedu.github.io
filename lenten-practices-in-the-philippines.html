Answer:

Marinduque is a small heart-shaped island in the Southern Tagalog, which, coincidentally, is also in the center of the Philippines. 3. How can you practice almsgiving this Lenten season?


Answer:

Almsgiving

The foundational call of Christians to charity is a frequent theme of the Gospels. During Lent, we are asked to focus more intently on "almsgiving," which means donating money or goods to the poor and performing other acts of charity. As one of the three pillars of Lenten practice, almsgiving is "a witness to fraternal charity" and "a work of justice pleasing to God." (Catechism of the Catholic Church, no. 2462).

16. Guide Questions: How do you live out in your own life the Lenten practices of prayer, fasting, and dimsgiving? What have you learned about yourself, your faith, and God through your own Lenten practices?


Answer:

The Lenten season is a time for reflection, self-examination, and spiritual renewal. As a believer, I strive to live out the Lenten practices of prayer, fasting, and thanksgiving in my own life. Through these practices, I have learned more about myself, my faith, and God.

Prayer is an essential part of my Lenten practice. I make it a point to set aside time each day for prayer, whether it's through attending Mass, reciting the Rosary, or simply meditating on Scripture. Through prayer, I have learned to rely more on God, to trust in His love and mercy, and to ask for His guidance in all aspects of my life.

Fasting is another Lenten practice that I try to incorporate into my life. I usually choose to fast from something that I enjoy, such as sweets or social media, as a way to detach myself from worldly pleasures and focus more on my spiritual life. Through fasting, I have learned to discipline myself and to control my desires, which has helped me to grow in self-awareness and humility.

Lastly, thanksgiving is an important aspect of my Lenten practice. I make it a point to reflect on the blessings in my life and express gratitude for them. Through thanksgiving, I have learned to appreciate the simple things in life and to see the hand of God in all things.

Through these Lenten practices, I have learned more about myself, my faith, and God. I have learned that I am capable of more than I thought, that my faith can sustain me through difficult times, and that God is always present and guiding me. Lent has helped me to deepen my relationship with God and to live my life with more purpose and meaning.

26. Which traditional Lenten practices make your heart restless? Why?​


Answer:

Lent is a solemn religious observance in the Christian liturgical calendar that begins on Ash Wednesday and ends approximately six weeks later, the night before Easter Sunday. The purpose of Lent is the preparation of the believer for Easter through prayer, doing penance, mortifying the flesh, repentance of sins, almsgiving, simple living and self-denial. This season is observed in the Anglican, Eastern Orthodox, Lutheran, Methodist, Moravian, Oriental Orthodox, Reformed, United Protestant and Roman Catholic Churches. Some Anabaptist, Baptist and nondenominational Christian churches also observe Lent.
