15. a process of synthesizing proteins​


Answer:

→This process is called protein synthesis, and it actually consists of two processes — transcription and translation. In eukaryotic cells, transcription takes place in the nucleus. During transcription, DNA is used as a template to make a molecule of messenger RNA (mRNA). The molecule of mRNA then leaves the nucleus and goes to a ribosome in the cytoplasm, where translation occurs. During translation, the genetic code in mRNA is read and used to make a protein. These two processes are summed up by the central dogma of molecular biology: DNA → RNA → Protein.

22. how is atp synthesized?​


Answer:

the ATP synthase is a mitochondrial enzyme localized in the inner membrane, where is catalyzes the synthesis of ATP from ADP and phosphate, driven by a flux of protons across a gradient generated by electron transfer from the proton chemically positive to the negative side

Answer:

The ATP synthase (or F1F0 ATPase and also referred to as complex V) uses the free energy of an electrochemical gradient of protons (or sodium ions) generated by the respiratory chain to synthesize ATP. The ATP synthases comprise a very large group of highly conserved enzymes that are found in the bacterial cytoplasmic membranes, the thylakoid membranes of chloroplasts, and the inner membranes of mitochondria. Most members of the group use H+ as the coupling ion (the Propionigenium modestum enzyme is an example of the few ATP synthases that can use Na+ as the physiological coupling ion).

ATP synthase complex (FoF1-ATP synthase). This complex allows protons to flow back into the matrix and uses the free energy change from this process to synthesize ATP from ADP and inorganic phosphate (Pi). It is located in knob-shaped structures embedded in the cristae (invaginations of the inner mitochondrial membrane) and extending into matrix. The Fo protein (the “o” in Fo refers to its sensitivity to oligomycin, a poison that blocks the flow of protons) extends through the inner mitochondrial membrane and serves as the proton channel between the intermembrane space and the matrix. The ATP synthase (F1-ATPase) is attached to the Fo protein on the inside of the matrix. ATP synthase uses the protons flowing into the matrix to bind ADP and Pi and release ATP. The F1-ATPase is named by the reverse reaction it catalyzes when it is isolated from mitochondria and thus uncoupled from the proton gradient.

29. Explain advantages and Disadvantages of synthesizing informationCite examples of advantage and disadvantage of synthesizing information​


Answer:

please give me brainliest

Synthesizing information involves taking different pieces of information and integrating them to create a comprehensive understanding or perspective on a particular topic. There are several advantages and disadvantages of synthesizing information:

Advantages:

1.Creates a comprehensive understanding: Synthesizing information allows individuals to create a comprehensive understanding of a particular topic by integrating different perspectives and viewpoints.

2.Saves time: By synthesizing information, individuals can save time as they do not need to read or research every single piece of information available on a topic.

3.Helps identify patterns and trends: Synthesizing information allows individuals to identify patterns and trends that may not be apparent when looking at individual pieces of information.

4.Facilitates decision-making: A comprehensive understanding of a topic can help individuals make informed decisions.

Disadvantages:

1.Bias: Synthesizing information can lead to bias if individuals only select information that supports their preconceived notions or beliefs.

2.Misinterpretation: Synthesizing information can lead to misinterpretation if individuals do not fully understand the different perspectives and viewpoints.

3.Overgeneralization: Synthesizing information can lead to overgeneralization if individuals make assumptions based on limited information.

4.Inaccuracies: Synthesizing information can lead to inaccuracies if individuals misinterpret or misunderstand the information they are synthesizing.

Example of advantage:

A student writing a research paper on the causes of climate change could synthesize information from different sources such as scientific research papers, news articles, and government reports. By integrating the information, the student can gain a comprehensive understanding of the different factors that contribute to climate change and the impacts of those factors.

Example of disadvantage:

A journalist writing a news article on a controversial topic could synthesize information from biased sources that support their preconceived notions or beliefs. This could result in the journalist presenting a one-sided perspective that does not accurately represent all viewpoints on the topic
