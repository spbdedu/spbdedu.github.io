6. Red squirrels and gray squirrels have been the native species on an island for many years. A few years ago, black squirrels entered this island. The black squirrel is an invasive species. Invasive species aren’t native to the ecosystem. They move into an ecosystem because of natural changes or human intervention. Red squirrels and black squirrels have identical food requirements, but gray squirrels have a different diet. What three effects will these events have on the island’s squirrel population?


1. If the new ecosystem they moved to does not have the gray squirrels' required diet, it's highly possible that this species will dwindle in numbers and die out.

2. If in case the opposite is true, and the red and black ones do not have a source for their diet, these two species might die out in that ecosystem.

3. If the first one is true, then it's possible that there may be a shortage of that diet, since two species sharing the same environment require the same diet. (Some hostility may form between the two due to limited sources.)

15. This squirrel lives in a forest and eats only acorns. What will MOST LIKELY happen to the squirrel if the oak trees in this forest start dying of a disease?


It would be tempting to say that the squirrel will die of starvation, but we all know that wild animals are quite tenacious and would do anything to survive. So, in the unfortunate event that oak trees in the forest start dying from a particular disease, the squirrel will move to another place, but of course, some or many may die due to starvation or the inability to hide from predators in the new environment.
