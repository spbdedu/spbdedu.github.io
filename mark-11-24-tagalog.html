2. Give a short "essay" about Mark 11:24​


Mark 11:24 is a verse from the Gospel of Mark in the New Testament of the Christian Bible. The verse reads, "Therefore I tell you, whatever you ask for in prayer, believe that you have received it, and it will be yours." This verse is often interpreted as a call to faith and trust in God's power to answer prayers.

The context of this verse is important to understanding its meaning. In the preceding verses, Jesus had just cursed a fig tree that had no fruit, and the tree withered and died. This event was seen as a symbolic demonstration of Jesus' power and authority. Later on, Jesus tells his disciples that they too have the power to perform miracles if they have faith in God.

In verse 24, Jesus is essentially telling his disciples that if they ask for something in prayer and truly believe that God will grant their request, then it will happen. This verse has been interpreted in a number of ways by Christians throughout history. Some see it as a call to faith and trust in God's power, while others see it as a promise of material blessings for those who have enough faith.

Regardless of how one interprets this verse, it is clear that it emphasizes the importance of faithin prayer. It encourages believers to approach God with confidence and to trust that He will answer their prayers in accordance with His will. However, it is important to note that this does not mean that God will grant every request made in prayer. God's answers to prayer are not always what we expect or desire, but they are always in our best interest and in accordance with His plan.

In summary, Mark 11:24 is a verse that emphasizes the importance of faith in prayer. It encourages believers to trust in God's power and to approach Him with confidence, believing that He will answer their prayers. However, it is important to keep in mind that God's answers to prayer may not always be what we expect or desire, but they are always in accordance with His will.

Mark 11:24 is a verse in the Bible that says, "Therefore I tell you, whatever you ask for in prayer, believe that you have received it, and it will be yours." This verse is often cited by Christians as a source of inspiration and encouragement to have faith in God and trust that he will answer their prayers.

At its core, Mark 11:24 is a call to faith and trust in God. It emphasizes the importance of believing that God will answer our prayers and grant us the things we ask for, as long as we have faith and trust in him. This verse also reminds us that prayer is a powerful tool that we can use to connect with God and ask for his help and guidance in our lives.

However, it's important to note that Mark 11:24 is not a guarantee that God will always grant our prayers exactly as we ask them. Sometimes, God's answer to our prayers may be different than what we expect or want. But even in these situations, we can trust that God has a plan for our lives and that he will work all things together for our good.

Overall, Mark 11:24 is a powerful reminder of the importance of faith, trust, and prayer in the lives of Christians. It encourages us to turn to God in times of need and to believe that he will answer our prayers according to his will and for our ultimate good

15. Reflection in Tagalog og Mark 1:1-8


Ang ulat ng Bibliya sa Marcos 1:1-8 ay tungkol kay Juan Bautista at bautismo ng mga Judio.  Si Juan na Tagapagbautismo ay nangaral sa iláng ng Judea bago mabautismuhan si Jesus, . Sinasabi niya sa mga Judio na sila ay magsisi sa kanilang mga kasalanan, sapagkat ang kaharian ng langit ay malapit na. Maraming Judio ang nakarinig sa sinabi ni Juan at nagbigay ng pansin dito. Ipinagtapat nila ang kanilang mga kasalanan, pinagsisihan ito, at pagkatapos ay nagpabautismo kay Juan Bautista sa Ilog Jordan.  

Aral ng Bautismo ng mga Judio

Ang mga Judio ay nagpabautismo dahil sa mga sumusunod na dahilan:

Inaamin nila ang kanilang mga nagawang pagkakamali. Pinagsisihan ng mga Judio ang mga nagawa nilang pagkakamali laban sa Kautusang Mosaiko. Sa simbolo ng bautismo, ipinakita nila ang kagustuhan at kaseryosohan ng mga pagbabagong gagawin sa kanilang buhay. Ipinakikita na ng mga Judio na nakahanda na silang tanggapin ang Anak ng Diyos, si Jesus, na isinugo sa lupa.

Iba pang Bautismo

Iba pang bautismo na binabanggit sa Bibliya :

bautismo sa tubig ni Jesus at ng kaniyang mga tagasunod ( Mat 3:13-15)bautismo kay Kristo Jesus at sa kaniyang kamatayan (Marcos 10:38, 39 )bautismo sa apoy (Mateo 3:7 )
