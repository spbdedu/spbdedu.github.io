It is commonly measured as the number of cases of HIV infection per 100,000 individuals within a specific population over a given time period. The morbidity rate of asthma refers to the frequency and severity of illness or disease related to asthma in a given population within a specific time period. Morbidity rate is typically measured using various indicators, such as the number of asthma-related hospitalizations, emergency room visits, or missed school or work days due to asthma.

The morbidity rate of asthma can vary depending on factors such as geographic location, age group, socioeconomic status, access to healthcare, and environmental triggers. In some populations, asthma morbidity may be higher due to factors such as air pollution, allergens, tobacco smoke exposure, or lack of access to appropriate asthma management and education.
